The development version of Neutron recently switched the default image editor from Pixlr to Phoenix. Even though I like Pixlr better, its API seems to poop out often. I actually had this same problem several years ago with Pixlr with another project and had to switch off it then too. With that said, I thought would write a summary of all the online photo editors I looked at why I chose Phoenix.

To start, here were the criteria I used to pick a photo editor for Neutron:

  • Advanced Tools: The editor needed to have more than just cropping and effects. I wanted an editor that could be used seriously for daily development tasks.
  • Transparency and Layers: The editor should be able to handle PNG transparency and layers. PNGs are just about all I work with nowadays if I have a say in it.
  • API: The editor needed to have a simple API to get images into the service and then save them back to Neutron IDE.
With these criteria I narrowed down the choices to the following editors:
  • Picnik: http://www.picnik.com/
  • Pixlr: http://pixlr.com/
  • Sumo Paint: http://www.sumopaint.com/home/
  • Splashup: http://www.splashup.com/
  • Phoenix: http://www.aviary.com/online/image-editor

Picnik

Picnik (now owned by Google) is very easy to use and has a great API, but unless you pay for the premium account, you don't get support for layers. I really wanted to stay away from a paid service if I could for default integration into Neutron IDE. However, even with the premium service, it did not handle PNG transparency and I couldn't figure out how to use layers easily.

Pixlr

Pixlr had everything I was looking for, but it seems that their API is hit or miss. I had the same problem in the past and tried them out again hoping this would not be the case now but it still was.

Sumo Paint

Sumo Paint was another great and highly advanced online photo editor. Sumo Paint has an API, but requires user interaction to setup. For Neutron IDE's purposes I was hoping to avoid this, but I highly recommend this photo editor.

Splashup

Splashup again was another great and highly advanced online photo editor, but no API. This editor is another highly recommended tool to try though.

Phoenix

Phoenix seemed to offer all the functionality needed and has a decent API. When importing tranparent PNGs, it does add a white background layer, but it is trivial to delete the layer and continue editing.

Conclusions

Phoenix definitely rose from the ashes and beat all the rest. However, several of these editors are still great if your not taking into consideration an API and integrating them into another project. Leave a comment and let me know what your favorite is.
