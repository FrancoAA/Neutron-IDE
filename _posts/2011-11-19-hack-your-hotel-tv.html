One of my favorite things to do when I go on trips is to bring my Roku. It is small and fits easily in any laptop bag, and then when I get to my hotel I can watch Netflix, play music via Pandora, or my watch own videos with the My Media Channel. This beats having to pay high prices for lame hotel videos you don't want to watch in the first place.

Most hotel TVs have RCA or HDMI inputs and free wireless, so we are all ready to go right?! Sometimes it is not that easy. Some hotel TVs will try to lock you out of switching inputs by not providing an input button on your remote or by switching back to the hotel input automatically.

To switch inputs first look for buttons on your TV. Most TVs have manual buttons, your just going to just get off your butt and look for them. If there are no buttons, your going to have to get a universal remote. I never ran into a TV with no buttons, but I heard some hotels do this to prevent switching off their input.

If you have the problem of the TV switching back to the hotel cable input, then you need to look in back of the TV. There will be a ethernet or smaller phone like wire. Unplug the wire and usually that does the trick of the TV automatically switching back. If the problem still occurs go ahead an unplug any other inputs from the hotel.

If anyone has any other tips or tricks please comment below. And have fun with having your media on the go!
